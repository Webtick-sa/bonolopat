Practical Assessment Task (PAT) Research

How Does Online Shopping Impact Consumers in South Africa?

An investigation into the positive and negative effects of modern digital commerce on local consumers.

Prepared by: Bonolo Teffo | Grade 12A | Seemahale Secondary School | Year 2026

▪ Abstract

This digital research project presents the synthesized outcomes of the Practical Assessment Task (PAT) Phase 3, executed in adherence to the guidelines on page 30 of the "Computer Applications Technology PAT GR 12 2026 Le_260105_200749.pdf". With the rapid acceleration of smartphone and internet infrastructure in South Africa, online transactions have become an essential component of modern retail. This research analyzes consumer behavior patterns, positive conveniences, critical online security vulnerabilities, and systemic solutions to support a safer digital shopping environment.

▪ Task Definition

What is the current situation?

Online shopping has become increasingly popular in South Africa due to the growth of internet access, smartphones and e-commerce platforms. Many consumers now prefer to buy goods online because it is convenient, time-saving and often offers a wider variety of products. However, despite these advantages, online shopping also presents several challenges such as online fraud, scams, delivery issues, poor product quality and privacy concerns.

What is the focus & purpose of this investigation?

The focus of this project is to explore how e-commerce platforms, specifically focusing on localized platforms like Takealot and global giants like Temu, Shein, and Amazon, affect consumer purchasing habits, decision-making, and financial security. The desired outcome is to establish a balance between modern e-commerce utility and consumer exposure to risk.

Focus Question: "How does online shopping impact consumers in South Africa?"
