Empirical Research & Data Interpretation

E-Commerce Trends & Survey Data Analysis

A comprehensive breakdown of South African consumer habits, popular platforms, and transaction vulnerabilities.

▪ Survey Methodology & Demographics

To compile qualitative local insights, a digital survey was administered to a cross-section of 25 local respondents, carefully processed within the spreadsheet file OnlineShoppingAnalysis.xlsx in compliance with the evaluation principles outlined on page 30 of the Computer Applications Technology PAT GR 12 2026 Le_260105_200749.pdf guide. The core inquiry directly supports the findings drafted in phase 1 report Bonolo Teffo.docx and finalized inside Report Bonolo Teffo.docx.

Below are the primary findings detailing the exact online services used by South African consumers and the major procedural hurdles experienced during transaction phases.

▪ Most Used Online Shopping Platforms

The survey results confirm that budget-conscious global platforms are gaining substantial ground alongside established domestic service leaders like Takealot.

Shopping Platform Respondent Count Percentage Share
Temu 9 36.0%
Shein 6 24.0%
Takealot 6 24.0%
Amazon 4 16.0%

▪ Problems Experienced During Online Shopping

Consumers reported experiencing critical transactional problems. Strikingly, payment system issues and scam/fraud attempts emerge as the top threats reported locally.

Friction Category Count Proportion of Friction
Scam/Fraud attempts 4 33.3%
Delayed delivery 1 8.3%
Wrong product delivered 0 0.0%
Poor product quality 3 25.0%
Payment processing issues 4 33.3%

▪ Synthesis with Research Literature

Synthesizing local survey patterns against global indicators found in E-commerce worldwide - statistics & facts _ Statista.pdf and How Ecommerce has Changed Consumer Behaviour - ESW.pdf illustrates that South African consumers are experiencing a double-edged sword. While global scale permits incredibly low price tags (such as on Temu and Shein), consumers undergo heightened exposure to poor quality merchandise and payment gateway friction.
